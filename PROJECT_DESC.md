# CSC 502 – Project

## Group Policy

Forming a group is recommended. Groups of up to 4 students are allowed.
All group members are expected to contribute meaningfully to the project.

## Overview

This is an exploratory research project focused on studying, explaining, and implementing
one or more important research papers related to a big data problem that interests you.

## Submission Deadline

Submit a **one-paragraph project description** along with the **names and student IDs**
of all group members no later than **March 19**.

## Project Phases

Your project can consist of the following three phases:

1. **Paper Understanding and Explanation**
   A clear explanation of the problem studied and the algorithm(s) proposed in the
   paper(s), possibly supported by a small real or toy dataset.

2. **Implementation**
   Implementation of the main algorithm(s) described in the paper(s), or a
   well-justified subset of them.

3. **Experimentation**
   Experimental evaluation of the algorithm(s) using real datasets.

> At least one of these phases must be non-trivial.

## Report

Submit a written report describing your work.

**Expected length:**

- 5–10 pages in double column IEEE format, or
- 12–15 pages in LNCS format

**Templates:**

- [IEEE Conference Template (Overleaf)](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/ieee-conference-template/grfzhhncsfqn)
- [Springer LNCS Template (Overleaf)](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/springer-lecture-notes-in-computerscience/kzwwpvhwnvfj)

## Presentation

Each group will present their project in class.

## Paper Selection

A set of papers is provided for you to choose from. You are also free to select
paper(s) related to your own research interests, including papers not in the
provided set.
